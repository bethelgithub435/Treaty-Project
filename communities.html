The Communities of Treaty 1 Territory

Treaty 1 is home to several First Nations communities each with its own unique culture. Learn about the communities that are part of Treaty 1 and their contributions to the region.

First Nations in Treaty 1

The following nations are part of Treaty 1, representing their unique cultures and traditions:

  • Peguis First Nation: Anishinaabe (Ojibway) and Cree
  • Brokenhead Ojibway Nation: Anishinaabe (Ojibway)
  • Long Plain First Nation: Anishinaabe (Ojibway)
  • Roseau River Anishinaabe First Nation: Anishinaabe (Ojibway)
  • Sandy Bay Ojibway Nation: Anishinaabe (Ojibway)

Treaty 1 Today

Treaty 1 continues to shape relationships today through modern implementation and economic development.

A major example is the Naawi-Oodena (Kapyong) Urban Reserve. This project, along with others like the Long Plain First Nation gas station, allows Treaty communities to create jobs and build sustainable economies within urban centers.

Treaty 1 Today

Treaty 1 continues to shape relationships today. Some modern examples include urban reserves and economic development projects.

Examples include the Kapyong Urban Reserve and businesses owned by Treaty 1 communities. These projects support economic growth and self-determination for First Nations.

Peguis First Nation

Peguis First Nation formerly known as St. Peter's Band, is a First Nations community located in Manitoba, Canada. It is one of the largest First Nations in the province and is part of Treaty 1. The community has a rich history and culture, with a strong connection to the land and traditional practices. Peguis First Nation continues to play an important role in the region, contributing to the cultural diversity and economic development of Manitoba.
