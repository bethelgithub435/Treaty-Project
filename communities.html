First Nations in Treaty 1

The following First Nations are part of Treaty 1:

  • Peguis First Nation
  • Brokenhead Ojibway Nation
  • Long Plain First Nation
  • Roseau River Anishinaabe First Nation
  • Sandy Bay Ojibway Nation

Treaty 1 Today

Treaty 1 continues to shape relationships today. Some modern examples include urban reserves and economic development projects.

Examples include the Kapyong Urban Reserve and businesses owned by Treaty 1 communities. These projects support economic growth and self-determination for First Nations.

Peguis First Nation

Peguis First Nation formerly known as St. Peter's Band, is a First Nations community located in Manitoba, Canada. It is one of the largest First Nations in the province and is part of Treaty 1. The community has a rich history and culture, with a strong connection to the land and traditional practices. Peguis First Nation continues to play an important role in the region, contributing to the cultural diversity and economic development of Manitoba.
